The **skills document (DC)** presents a candidate in a structured, well-laid-out format, ready to share with your clients.

## Automatic creation

By default, a setting **automatically creates a skills document when a candidate is created**, from the **default template** — saving time on every add.

<img src="/images/dossier-creation-auto.png" alt="Setting to automatically create the skills document from the default template" width="700" />

You can enable or disable this behavior in the [settings](/en/fonctionnalites/dossier/parametres).

## Edit the document

On a skills document page, you **edit the document like in Word** — simply and intuitively: add **columns**, **fields**, reorganize the content, and so on.

## Multiple documents per candidate

You can use **versioning** and create **several skills documents for the same candidate**. A document can also be **linked to a need**. See [Versioning](/en/fonctionnalites/dossier/versionning).

## Templates

At onboarding, you have a **default template**. You can create **other templates** if you wish: find them in your **organization settings**, under **Templates and documents**. See [Document templates](/en/fonctionnalites/templates/documents).

## Statuses

A skills document goes through three statuses:

- **Draft** — the default status.
- **Pending** — sent to the candidate, not yet validated.
- **Validated** — validated by the candidate.
