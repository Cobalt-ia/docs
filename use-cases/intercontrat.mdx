Intercontract time is the most controllable cost in an IT staffing operation — and one of the most damaging when left unmanaged. Every day a consultant spends on bench is direct margin erosion: salary paid, no revenue generated. Most ESN firms treat intercontract as reactive: the mission ends, then the scramble starts. Cobalt flips that model. It tracks every mission end date, predicts upcoming gaps 60 days out, and prepares targeted proposals before a consultant ever leaves their current engagement.

## The 72-Hour Repositioning Playbook

<Steps>
  <Step title="Day 0, 9:00 — Mission ends. Cobalt is already prepared.">
    Cobalt flagged this mission ending 60 days ago and has been monitoring it since. The moment the mission closes, Cobalt scans your entire client portfolio and surfaces the 8 accounts where the consultant's profile is the strongest fit — based on past missions, tech stack, sector, and client history.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Day 0, 14:00 — AI-generated proposals ready to review">
    For each of the 8 matched accounts, Cobalt drafts a contextualized outreach email and prepares an anonymized, branded skills document. Your business manager reviews and approves — nothing goes to a client without a human in the loop. They're ready to send before end of day.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Day 1 — Your business manager is fully armed">
    When the business manager picks up their first client call, they have the full CRM context in front of them: account history, the feedback from the consultant's last mission there, strengths to highlight, and the pre-drafted proposal. Every conversation is informed, not improvised.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Day 3 — Mission confirmed">
    Replies are tracked directly in the CRM. For accounts that haven't responded, automated follow-up sequences handle the next touch. Your team focuses on closing, not chasing.
  </Step>
</Steps>
