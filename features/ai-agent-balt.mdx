Balt is Cobalt's AI sourcing agent. Instead of writing boolean queries or juggling multiple platforms, you describe the candidate you need in plain language — the same way you'd brief a headhunter — and Balt returns a ranked, ready-to-contact shortlist in under two minutes. It searches your internal database and 800M+ public profiles simultaneously, scores every match across 50+ criteria, and explains every score so you always know why a candidate ranks where they do.

## How Balt Works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Describe your need in plain language">
    Type your search the way you'd describe it to a colleague: *"Senior DevOps engineer, 7+ years, Kubernetes and AWS, available in Paris within 30 days, open to permanent."* No boolean operators, no quoted strings, no wildcards — Balt understands intent and synonyms out of the box.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Balt searches your internal database and the web">
    Balt queries your existing Cobalt database and the open web at the same time. External sources include LinkedIn, GitHub, freelance portals (Malt, Upwork), and job boards. The combined pool covers 800M+ profiles, so no strong candidate slips through a sourcing gap.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Profiles are scored and ranked automatically">
    Every matching profile receives a transparent relevance score built from 50+ criteria. Skills carry the most weight, followed by availability, location, and seniority. You see the score breakdown for each candidate — not just a number, but the reasoning behind it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review your GDPR-compliant shortlist">
    Balt delivers a shortlist of 20–50 ranked candidates, each with an AI-generated summary covering skills, experience, fit score, and recommendations. All data is processed under EU hosting with end-to-end encryption and native consent management — your shortlist is ready to act on without any compliance overhead.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Key Capabilities

- **Natural language search** — describe the role as you would to a colleague; no query syntax required.
- **Transparent scoring across 50+ criteria** — every score is explainable, never a black box.
- **Dual-source coverage** — searches your internal Cobalt database and 800M+ public profiles (LinkedIn, GitHub, Malt, Upwork, and major job boards) in a single query.
- **Weighted scoring model** — skills & competencies 40%, availability 25%, location 20%, seniority 15%.
- **Shortlist of 20–50 ranked candidates** — ready to contact, with contact details surfaced via enrichment.
- **AI profile summaries** — each card shows skills, experience highlights, fit score, and actionable recommendations.
- **Saves 3–5 hours of manual sourcing per role** — spend your time on conversations, not searches.

## Frequently Asked Questions

<Accordion title="Is Balt different from boolean search?">
  Yes. Boolean search requires exact keyword matches and technical query syntax. Balt understands intent and synonyms — searching for *"Senior cloud engineer"* also matches *"Lead Engineer AWS/GCP"*, *"Platform Engineer"*, and similar titles. You never need to write complex operators, quoted phrases, or wildcard patterns.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Does Balt replace LinkedIn Recruiter?">
  Balt complements LinkedIn rather than replacing it. While LinkedIn Recruiter is limited to LinkedIn profiles, Balt simultaneously searches GitHub, freelance portals (Malt, Upwork), job boards, and your internal Cobalt database. If you prefer to start on LinkedIn, the Cobalt Chrome Extension lets you import profiles directly from any LinkedIn page into your Cobalt lists.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How long does a shortlist take?">
  Between 30 seconds and 2 minutes for a shortlist of 20–50 ranked candidates. Search time depends on query complexity and source coverage, but Balt is built to deliver results fast enough to use mid-client-call.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Is Balt GDPR-compliant?">
  Yes. All processing runs on EU infrastructure (France and Germany). Data is protected with end-to-end encryption, and Cobalt provides a Data Processing Agreement (DPA). Native consent management is built into the platform, so candidates' rights — including the right to deletion — are handled automatically.
</Accordion>
